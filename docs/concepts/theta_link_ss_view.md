# [iut:theta_link] — SS view
> 3-agent verify pending (SS-side draft).

## How SS describes Θ-link

- SS §2.1.9, p.9: "The Θ-link between two Hodge theaters HT1 and HT2 is the (full poly-)isomorphism
  between the F⊩×µ-prime strip F⊩×µ_Θ,1 constructed from the Θ-pilot object in HT1 and
  the F⊩×µ-prime strip F⊩×µ_q,2 constructed from the q-pilot object in HT2."
- SS p.9: "the choice of such an isomorphism identifies the corresponding abstract pilot objects"
- SS p.9: "the Θ-link forgets about the concrete embeddings of q̃_v into o_{k̄_v} and
  Θ_v ~ (q^{j²}_v)_{j=1,...,ℓ*} into o_{k̄_v}"
- Source: SS 2018, §2.1.9, p.9

## SS's reformulation

- SS p.9: "the F⊩×µ-prime strips are given by data of the form G_v ⟲ o^{×µ}_{k̄_v} × N on
  both sides (at finite places), and **this data is canonically the same on both sides**"
- SS p.9: "It is simply the name of the generator of the monoid N that appears that is called
  Θ respectively q."
- Implication: after canonical identification, the Θ-link amounts to renaming one generator as
  another — i.e. a tautological statement at the level of abstract prime strips.

## Where SS agrees

- Θ-link is well-defined as a (full poly-)isomorphism of F⊩×µ-prime strips (SS p.9)
- Abstract pilot objects on both sides are identified via the link (SS p.9)
- The underlying categorical data (G_v ⟲ o^{×µ}_{k̄_v} × N) is abstractly isomorphic on
  both sides (SS pp.8–9, §2.1.5)

## Where SS sees no essential content

- SS p.9: canonical isomorphism R⊙,Θ ≅ R⊙,q ≅ R via γ_can renders the identification
  trivial — "the data is canonically the same on both sides"
- SS p.10: "There is one consistent choice of isomorphisms given by using the natural
  isomorphisms R⊙,Θ ≅ R⊙,q ≅ R coming from the observation that the global realified
  Frobenioids coming from F⊩×µ-prime strips are always canonically trivial"
- SS p.10: with consistent identifications "one must in (1.5) omit the scalars j² that appear,
  which leads to an **empty inequality**"
- Footnote 12, p.9: "with the simplifications outlined above, such as identifying identical
  copies of objects along the identity, the critical [IUTT-3, Theorem 3.11] does not become
  false, but **trivial**"

## Critical caveat

SS does NOT redefine the Θ-link. They accept it as a poly-isomorphism of F⊩×µ-prime strips.
Their objection is that Mochizuki's interpretation — that HT1 and HT2 carry genuinely
distinct arithmetic data recoverable after the link — is unsupported: the abstract strips are
already canonically identified, so no non-trivial transport of arithmetic information occurs.
The gap therefore lies in the passage from abstract to concrete pilot objects (SS §2.2, pp.9–10),
not in the definition of the Θ-link itself.
