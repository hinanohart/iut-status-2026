# [iut:etale_theta] Étale Theta Function (Mochizuki-side draft)

> ⚠️ This document is the **mochizuki-side draft**. The neutral merge in `docs/section_1_prerequisites.md` is the consumer-facing view.
> Last verified: 2026-05-06

## Definition (Mochizuki / RIMS)

- The **étale theta function** (`iut:etale_theta`) is the Kummer class η̈^Θ ∈ H^1(Π^tp_{Ÿ}, Δ_Θ) of the classical formal algebraic theta function associated to a Tate curve over a nonarchimedean mixed-characteristic local field, where Π^tp_{Ÿ} is the tempered fundamental group of a certain covering Ÿ of the once-punctured elliptic curve and Δ_Θ is a cyclotomic module.
- A **mono-theta environment** [mod N] is a triple: (a) the topological group Π^tp_Y[μ_N]; (b) the subgroup D_Y ⊆ Out(Π^tp_Y[μ_N]) generated by the images of K× and Gal(Y/X) (≅ l·ℤ); (c) the μ_N-conjugacy class of subgroups determined by the image of the theta section s^Θ_{Ÿ}.
- Reference: Mochizuki, "The Étale Theta Function and its Frobenioid-theoretic Manifestations" (EtTh), Definition 2.13(ii), p. 43 (approx.); §1 (construction of η̈^Θ). URL: <https://www.kurims.kyoto-u.ac.jp/~motizuki/The%20Etale%20Theta%20Function%20and%20its%20Frobenioid-theoretic%20Manifestations.pdf>

## Key properties (statement-level)

- **Theorem 1.6 — Tempered Anabelian Rigidity**: Let γ : Π^tp_{X_α} →̃ Π^tp_{X_β} be an isomorphism of tempered fundamental groups of Tate curves (singular split stable reduction). Then: (i) γ(Π^tp_{Ÿ_α}) = Π^tp_{Ÿ_β}; (ii) γ induces an isomorphism (Δ_Θ)_α →̃ (Δ_Θ)_β compatible with the surjections H^1(G_{K̈}, Δ_Θ) →̃ ℤ from valuations; (iii) γ maps the class O×_{K̈_α}·η̈^Θ_α to a Π^tp_{X_β}/Π^tp_{Y_β}≅ℤ-conjugate of O×_{K̈_β}·η̈^Θ_β.
  - Reference: EtTh, Theorem 1.6, pp. 22–23.

- **Theorem 1.10 — Constant Multiple Rigidity**: Under the additional hypothesis that η̈^{Θ,Z} is of standard type (value ±1 at √{-1}), any γ preserves the standard sets of values of η̈^{Θ,Z}, and determines a {±1}-structure on the (K×)^∧-torsor at the unique cusp, compatible with the canonical integral structure and preserved by γ.
  - Reference: EtTh, Theorem 1.10, pp. 27–28.

- **Corollary 2.18 — Group-theoretic invariance of mono-theta environment**: The mono-theta environment is a group/category-theoretic invariant of the tempered étale fundamental group Π^tp_X, and also of a certain tempered Frobenioid associated to the curve.
  - Reference: EtTh, Corollary 2.18 (cited in Introduction, p. 3).

- **Three rigidity properties of the mono-theta environment** (summarized in EtTh Introduction, p. 3): (a) **cyclotomic rigidity** — the cyclotomic action is intrinsically determined; (b) **discrete rigidity** — the relevant period lattice is discrete (fails for the bi-theta environment: cf. Corollary 2.16); (c) **constant multiple rigidity** — the theta function is determined up to ±1, not up to an arbitrary unit multiple.
  - Reference: EtTh, Introduction pp. 3–5; Corollaries 2.18–2.19.

- **Theorem 5.10 — Category-theoreticity of Frobenioid-theoretic Theta Environments**: The tempered Frobenioid C over base category D associated to X^log is of rationally standard type; and the mono-theta environment constructed from C is isomorphic (as a mod N mono-theta environment) to the group-theoretically constructed mono-theta environment of §2, with cyclotomic rigidity isomorphisms coinciding.
  - Reference: EtTh, Theorem 5.10, pp. 96–97 (approx.); Proposition 5.1.

## Why this is a prerequisite for IUT

The `iut:etale_theta` supplies the "Θ" in the Θ-link. In IUTchI, the Θ-link relates Frobenioid-theoretic portions encoding nΘ_v (values of the theta function at bad primes) across distinct Hodge theaters (IUTchI, p. 17). The three rigidity properties of the mono-theta environment — especially cyclotomic rigidity and constant multiple rigidity — are what allow the Θ-link to carry controlled arithmetic information without collapsing to an indeterminate unit multiple. The mono-theta environment serves as a "translation apparatus" between the global arithmetic gap ℤ vs. ℤ̂ and the local extension structure of the tempered Frobenioid (EtTh, p. 6).

## Forbidden translations

- Do NOT translate to: perfectoid spaces, diamonds, condensed mathematics.
- Why: The mono-theta environment's rigidity properties rely on the non-abelian structure of the tempered fundamental group and on Kummer theory within that group; replacing them with perfectoid or condensed ring structures would destroy the discrete-rigidity distinction between mono- and bi-theta environments that is essential to the theory. (Cf. Mochizuki, "Mathematics of Mutually Alien Copies", §1.)
