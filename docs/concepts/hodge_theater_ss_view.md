# [iut:HodgeTheater] — Scholze-Stix view
> 3-agent verify pending (SS-side draft).

## How SS describes Hodge Theater

- A (Θ±ellNF-)Hodge theater is "a certain amount of data that abstractly comes from the fixed
  once-punctured elliptic curve X." (p.6)
- SS derive from Mochizuki's own results ([IUTT-1, Cor 6.12(i), Prop 6.6(iii), Cor 5.6(ii),
  Prop 4.8(ii), Def 6.13]) that the functor from the single-object category {X} to
  Θ±ellNF-Hodge theaters is an **equivalence of categories**. (p.6)
- Consequence: "choosing a Hodge theater is equivalent to choosing a once-punctured elliptic
  curve abstractly isomorphic to X." The equivalence is constructive. (p.6)
- "Étale-like data" = π₁(X) as abstract topological group (up to inner auto). (p.5)
- "Frobenius-like picture" = π₁(X) acting on a monoid (e.g. o×_{k̄v}); often categorically
  equivalent to a hyperbolic-curves category, "but not always." (p.5–6)
- Reference: SS 2018, pp. 5–6

## SS's reformulation and critique

- **Simplification claimed**: SS identify "choosing a Hodge theater" with "choosing a curve
  abstractly isomorphic to X." Mochizuki "was not able to convince us during the week why
  such a simplification was not allowed." (p.4, excuse (3))
- **Θ-link between HT₁ and HT₂**: Under SS's analysis, the F⊩×µ-prime strips on both sides
  are "data of the form Gv ↷ o×µ_{k̄v} × N … canonically the same on both sides. It is simply
  the name of the generator … that is called Θ respectively q." (p.9)
- **Monodromy objection** (core critique): Tracing all ordered 1-dim R-vector spaces in
  [IUTT-3, Cor 3.12], SS conclude that introducing j² scalars on the Θ-side makes the
  commutative diagram have "monodromy j², i.e. being inconsistent." With consistent
  identifications the j² factors must be dropped, yielding an empty inequality. (p.10)
- **Full poly-isomorphisms**: SS spent "a lot of time" on this; Mochizuki "was not able to
  explain this convincingly in our opinion." (p.7, fn.8)
- Reference: SS 2018, pp. 4, 7, 9–10

## Where SS agrees

- Mochizuki's anabelian theorem ([Anab3, Thm 1.9, Cor 1.10]) is accepted as valid. (p.5)
- The categorical structure of Hodge theaters in IUTT-1 is not contested; SS work within it.
- Some simplifications confirmed by Mochizuki in Kyoto as "OK." (p.4, excuse (1))
- The log-link and Θ-link constructions as categorical operations are not themselves disputed.

## Where SS sees no essential content

- Anabelian geometry in IUTT does not supply isomorphisms of fundamental groups beyond
  those from isomorphisms of schemes (Remark 9, p.5).
- The two distinct Hodge theaters, under consistent identifications, carry no data not already
  in the fixed curve X.
- The final inequality reduces to "0 ≤ d(P)", which is "essentially free of content." (p.4)

## Critical caveat

- SS do **not** redefine Hodge Theater; they derive an equivalence from Mochizuki's own cited
  results and argue the distinct copies add no new information.
- The objection targets the **use** of distinct copies in [IUTT-3, Cor 3.12], not the internal
  validity of a single Hodge theater.
- SS are explicit: "even with all subtleties restored, the issue … will prevail." (p.4)
