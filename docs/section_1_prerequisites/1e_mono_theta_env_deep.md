## Recap: iut:mono_theta_environment (ж—ўзҷ»йҢІгҖҒexpand)

**Source**: EtTh В§2, Definition 2.13 (pp. 43вҖ“44)

A **[mod N] mono-theta environment** is an ordered triple
`M = (О , D_О , s^Оҳ_О )` where:

- `(a)` `О ` is a topological group isomorphic to `О ^tp_Y[Ој_N]`;
- `(b)` `D_О  вҠҶ Out(О )` is a subgroup corresponding to `D_Y` (generated by
  the images of `KГ—` and `Gal(Y/X) вү… lВ·Z`);
- `(c)` `s^Оҳ_О ` is the `Ој_N`-conjugacy class of subgroups of `О ` determined
  by the image of the theta section `s^Оҳ_Её : О ^tp_Её вҶ’ О ^tp_Y[Ој_N]`.

A **model** mono-theta environment is the specific triple
`M_N = (О ^tp_Y[Ој_N], D_Y, s^Оҳ_Её)` constructed from a fixed Г©tale theta
function; a general mono-theta environment is any triple isomorphic to a model.

Core property: three rigidities вҖ” cyclotomic, discrete, constant multiple вҖ”
all established in Cor. 2.19.


## 1e.1 Cyclotomic rigidity (`iut:mono_theta_cyclotomic_rigidity`)

**Source**: EtTh Cor. 2.19 (i) (p. 58); cf. also Thm. 1.10

**Statement (condensed)**: Given a mod N mono-theta environment
`MвҖў = (О вҖў, D_{О вҖў}, s^Оҳ_{О вҖў})` isomorphic to `M_N`, there exists a
*functorial group-theoretic algorithm* вҖ” invoking only the abstract
structure of `MвҖў`, functorial with respect to isomorphisms of mono-theta
environments, and devoid of any reference to any identification `MвҖў вү… M_N`
вҖ” that produces:

1. Subquotients `О вҖў|(lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ) вҠҶ О вҖў|(О”вҖў_Y)_Оҳ вҠҶ О вҖў|(О вҖў_Y)_Оҳ` of `О вҖў`
   compatible with the corresponding subquotients of `О ^tp_Y[Ој_N]`.
2. **Two splittings** of the natural surjection
   `О вҖў|(lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ) вҶ  (lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ)`,
   whose difference yields a canonical isomorphism of cyclotomes
   `(lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ) вҠ— (Z/NZ) вү… О вҖў_Ој := Ker(О вҖў|(lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ) вҶ  (lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ))`.

Any isomorphism `MвҖў вү… M_N` maps these splittings to the two splittings
determined by `s^alg_Её` and `s^Оҳ_Её`.

**Significance**: The cyclotome `Ој_N` is identified *intrinsically* from
`MвҖў` alone. This is what makes the theta Kummer class "self-contained" вҖ”
one can read off the cyclotomic twist from `MвҖў` without auxiliary choices.
Thm. 1.10 is the `В§1` precursor (scheme-theoretic version for a single
isomorphism `Оі`).


## 1e.2 Discrete rigidity (`iut:mono_theta_discrete_rigidity`)

**Source**: EtTh Cor. 2.19 (ii) (p. 59)

**Statement (condensed)**: Let `E вҠҶ NвүҘ1` be a cofinal, totally ordered
subset with `1 вҲҲ E`, giving a natural projective system of model
mono-theta environments `{M_M; ОІ*_{M',M}}_{M,M'вҲҲE}`.

Any projective system `{MвҖў_M; Оі*_{M',M}}_{M,M'вҲҲE}` of (abstract)
mono-theta environments (with `MвҖў_M` a mod `M` mono-theta environment)
**is isomorphic** to the natural projective system вҖ” i.e., there exist
isomorphisms `Оұ_M : M_M вү… MвҖў_M` compatible with all transition maps.

**Significance**: The Г©tale theta classes in a projective system of
mono-theta environments determine a *single discrete `lВ·Z`-torsor* in the
projective limit, not a profinite one. The key arithmetic fact is:
`Z/Z вү  0`, i.e., `Z` is not profinitely complete вҖ” so "discrete" here
means the torsor lives over `lВ·Z` (discrete) rather than over its
profinite completion `lВ·бәҗ`. Contrast: bi-theta environments fail this
(Cor. 2.16 / Remark 2.16.1), because their extra symmetry introduces an
`lВ·бәҗ`-indeterminacy in the projective limit.


## 1e.3 Constant multiple rigidity (`iut:mono_theta_constant_multiple_rigidity`)

**Source**: EtTh Cor. 2.19 (iii) (p. 59); cf. Thm. 1.10

**Statement (condensed)**: Suppose `О·МҲ^{Оҳ,lВ·ZГ—Ој_2}` is of standard type.
Let `О вҖў_X` be a topological group isomorphic to `О ^tp_X`. There exists a
*functorial group-theoretic algorithm* вҖ” invoking only the abstract
topological group structure of `О вҖў_X`, functorial with respect to
isomorphisms, devoid of reference to any fixed identification `О вҖў_X вү… О ^tp_X`
вҖ” for constructing a collection of classes in
`HВ№(О вҖў_Её, (lВ·О”вҖў_Оҳ))`
such that any isomorphism `О вҖў_X вү… О ^tp_X` maps this collection to the
Г©tale theta classes `О·МҲ^{Оҳ,lВ·ZГ—Ој_2}` **up to multiplication by an
`l`-th root of unity** (Cor. 2.8 (i)).

Furthermore, given a projective system of mono-theta environments as in (ii),
the compatible system of these collections (combined with the cyclotomic
isomorphism of (i) and the theta sections of each `MвҖў_M`) yields a
projective system of **bi-theta environments** isomorphic to a natural
bi-theta system of standard type (Cor. 2.16).

**Significance**: The Г©tale theta function is reconstructible from `О ^tp_X`
up to `{Вұ1}` and `l`-th root of unity ambiguity вҖ” no larger indeterminacy.
This is the group-theoretic shadow of Thm. 1.10 (which works at the level of
a single isomorphism). The residual `Вұ1` ambiguity is *not* an error; it
corresponds to the canonical `{Вұ1}`-structure at the cusp (Thm. 1.10 (iii)).


## How these support IUT

- **IUTchII, В§2 Оё-link**: The Оё-link `Оҳ^Г—Ој` identifies the `q`-parameters
  across distinct Hodge theaters. Well-definedness requires that the Kummer
  class of the theta function can be transported through the link вҖ” which
  demands all three rigidities. Without cyclotomic rigidity the cyclotome
  comparison across the link is ambiguous; without discrete rigidity the
  projective-limit theta value drifts over `lВ·бәҗ`; without constant multiple
  rigidity the theta value itself is not pinned (only an `lВ·бәҗ`-orbit).

- **IUTchIII, Cor. 3.12 multiradial algorithm**: The multiradial
  representation of the theta Kummer classes uses Cor. 2.19 as the
  foundational rigidity input. Specifically, the algorithm must construct
  the theta values radially (= independently of which "spoke" of the
  log-theta lattice is chosen) вҖ” this independence is guaranteed by the
  functorial, choice-free nature of the algorithms in Cor. 2.19.

- **Mono vs. bi-theta distinction**: Only mono-theta environments satisfy
  all three rigidities simultaneously. Bi-theta environments fail discrete
  rigidity (Cor. 2.16). This asymmetry is non-trivial: a naГҜve
  "use both sections" approach introduces non-discrete indeterminacy that
  contaminates the Cor. 3.12 output.
